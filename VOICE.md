# Voice & Positioning

Source: Bio.docx (Guia de Bio & Posicionamento). Archetype: Sage (dominant) + Hero (secondary).

## Core Positioning

The bio is not a resume. It is a point of view. It answers the question every client asks unconsciously before any other: why does this photographer understand my project?

The central asset: not a photographer who learned architecture, but an architectural designer with 10+ years of experience who photographs. Every piece of written content orbits this fact.

## Bio Structure — Three Fixed Moves

Order is fixed, never inverted.

1. **Value proposition** — what the client gets, not the photographer's name first
2. **Credential** — the architectural design background, stated as context, not vanity
3. **Call to action** — direct, no pressure. The Sage does not beg, he invites.

## Bio Versions by Context

**Instagram (max 150 characters):**
Photographs architecture with the eye of someone who designs it. Designer for 10+ years. Portfolio below.
No hashtags, no camera emojis. Arrow points to the link, not the feed.

**Short bio (site / LinkedIn):**
Three sentences, three moves, no wasted words. Value proposition → credential → invitation to view the portfolio.

**Medium bio (about page / proposal):**
Can be split into three standalone paragraphs. Each paragraph works alone. Closes on: images that do not document architecture — they reveal it.

**Long bio (press kit / full about page):**
Opens with the founding question. Middle section on the design career. Closes by naming the audience directly: architects, developers, real estate professionals who want their work represented with the same seriousness it was conceived with.

## Outreach Tone by Segment

**To architects:** Collegiate, not salesy. Speak as an equal. Lead with shared design vocabulary, not a pitch.

**To real estate / developers:** Professional, value-oriented. Focus on what the client gains, not what the photographer does.

## Voice Rules

The Sage does not self-promote. He demonstrates. Every sentence should read as if written by someone who has more to say than they're saying, and chose to say only the essential.

**Avoid:** passionate about photography, capture unique moments, incredible photos, DM me, special prices, years of experience, dedicated professional.

**Use:** reveal, interpret, understand, communicate, the intent behind, what the project meant to say, with the eye of someone who designs, before raising the camera.

No exclamation points. No excessive emojis. No direct sales language.

## Manifesto

*Architecture is intention made space. To photograph it is to reveal that intention to those who were never there.*

Usable on: homepage, proposal header, first slide of any presentation.
